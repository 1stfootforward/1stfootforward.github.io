Feeling Good While Losing Weight

Losing excess weight is one of the hardest things you will do, just because its hard doesn't mean it shouldn’t be done. Below are a few tips to help you be more successful.

Set a Plan and stick to it

By setting a plan in place, that includes smart, measurable, achievable, realistic and time-framed goals. Write these goals down and stick them to your mirror, say your goals out loud too yourself every morning. As motivation is the key the more realistic your weekly goal is the more achievable it becomes, motivating you to achieve them.

Hydration

Water

Water is important and helps your body function at it’s best, it is necessary for all digestive, absorption, circulatory, and excretory functions. Water also helps maintain proper body temperature.

format_align_left Read Article

Water is the most critical nutrient you can give your body. Water is needed to balance all of the bodies systems, including the brain, heart, lungs, kidneys, and muscles. Mild dehydration is the main cause of daytime fatigue, by keeping up your water intake during the day, you will in turn feel better, more awake to get things done, like exercise. you will also feel less hungry. One of the main reason we feel hungry is that we are dehydrated, so the next time you have a glass of water before and reap the rewards.

Sleep

Sleep

We all know how important sleep is and how it effects our day if we don’t get enough. Not only does it play a huge role in brain function learning and retaining information sleep also is massive when we are trying to lose the extra weight, and is usually the key behind some people’s fitness journeys.

format_align_left Read Article

Sleep is very important for every fitness goal and health benefits. With weight loss though it plays a crucial role especially if you are training as well. Our bodies recover the most whilst we sleep, so if you are training hard and eating well, sleep may be the factor stopping you from achieving these goals. Being tired sets your brain to make bad decisions, like skipping training and bad food choices.

Meditation & Yoga

Meditation is a cost-free strategy, that is used to clear the mind, as you embark on your journey to a healthier you, you may find meditation a great tool to use. A lot of the time we can find ourselves eating extra when we are stressed to help calm ourselves down, however, you can eliminate this feeling be meditating for only 5 minutes a day. If you have never meditated before, I found including Yoga to my routine helped. Just by slowing down my training 2 days a week I found I was calmer and more relaxed, in turn when I transitioned to Meditating it was a lot easier to turn off and relax.
