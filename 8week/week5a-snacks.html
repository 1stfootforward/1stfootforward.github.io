Week 5

Pre and post workout Snacks

Sometimes you can find it hard to eat before going to the gym or working out, and in some cases, can’t stand the thought of it afterward. However, just like breakfast, these meals are just as important, the pre-snack, fueling our bodies with energy and the post-snack aids in the body’s recovery. Having a snack instead of a meal helps me when I don’t feel like eating. However, because I know it is really important, I make an effort.

Below, are some recipes that I found useful when I started out!



Pre Workout

By eating before your workout you increase your energy, which allows you to work out longer and at a higher intensity level. If you are inclined to early workouts than you don’t need to have a heavy meal, a lighter option will work just as well. You can opt for a low-fat yogurt or even a glass of fruit juice to raise your blood sugar levels.

Below are some easy to make and quick to grab and eat pre-workout snacks

Cacao Energy Balls

  • 1 cup Rolled oats
  • ½ cup Coconut oil (melted)
  • ½ cup Cacao powder
  • ½ cup Dates
  • ¼ cup Chia Seeds
  • Water

Blitz all ingredients together, except for the Chia seeds and Water. Add water only if needed to gain right consistency, roll into balls, then roll in the Chia seeds to coat.

Banana Peanut Butter Smash

Cut in halve one banana lengthways, spread with peanut Butter! Enjoy.

Trail Mix – raw energy

Walnuts, pumpkin seeds, sunflower seeds, cinnamon, nutmeg, sea salt, dried apricots.

Post Workout

The importance of eating after a workout is to restore the energy lost during the workout. Plus you want to rebuild muscle and fueling them with food is an excellent way to do that. You can rebuild the tissue that breaks down during a workout by eating within two hours after your workout. Many people like to down a protein-packed shake, which is a great way to rebuild.

Below are some of my favourite post workout snacks

Greek yoghurt topped with muesli and berries

In a cup, half full with Greek yoghurt, top with 2 Tbsp muesli and 5 berries.

Protein Muesli Bars

Using 2 cups of your already toasted muesli from last week, mix in ¼ cup of honey (melted). Spread into a slice tin leave to set in the fridge for at least 2 hours.

Banana berry smoothie

  • 1 banana
  • 1 cup frozen berries
  • ¼ cup oats
  • ½ cup milk
  • 1 tsp chia seeds
  • Blend all together and enjoy
