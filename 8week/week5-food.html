Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates get a bad rap, however we need them to help as function properly. You just need to choose wisely below are some complex and high fibre recipes to get you started. And as you fill out your meal plan. I will help you include these good carbs into the right part of the day when your bodies need them the most!

Complex Carbs

Stuffed Zucchini’s


  • 1 cup Quinoa cooked
  • ¼ cup baby spinach
  • 1/8 cup Walnuts
  • 1/8 cup Feta crumbled
  • ½ tsp Smoked paprika, plus additional for sprinkling on zucchini
  • ½ tsp Chili powder
  • Salt/pepper to taste
  • 2 large Zucchinis

  1. Cut each zucchini in half lengthwise and scrape out the seeds with a small spoon, to create a “boat.”
  2. Sprinkle each boat with salt, pepper and a pinch of smoked paprika.
  3. Cook in the oven @ 180 degrees for 10 minutes or fork soft.
  4. Remove from oven and top with Quinoa mix. This recipe is also great, topped with the avocado salsa.


Quinoa Salad

  • 2 cup Quinoa cooked
  • 1/2 cup baby spinach
  • 1/4 cup Walnuts
  • 1/4 cup Feta crumbled

Mix all together and serve.



Burrito bowls

  • 3 cups cooked brown rice
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • 1 can black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 2 avocados, lightly mashed (optional)
  • 3/4 cup salsa
  • ¼ cup corn (cooked)

  1. Stir lime juice into rice.
  2. Divide rice between four plates and then put the black beans next to the rice.
  3. Then mix corn and salsa together, and put next to the beans.
  4. Top with lettuce and sour cream if you desire.


High Fibre

Vegetable-packed Quinoa Cups

  • 1 cup cooked Quinoa
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 eggs
  • ½ cup red onions, broccoli, peppers and cheese
  • ¼ cup spring onions
  • Salt and pepper

  1. Combine all ingredients in a bowl and mix to combine, portion mixture evenly into 12 muffin tins.
  2. Bake for 15 minutes or until edges have come away from sides and are golden brown.


Pumpkin blue cheese salad

  • ¼ Pumpkin, diced
  • ¼ Red onion, sliced
  • 40 grams Blue Cheese, crumbled
  • ¼ cup Walnuts

  1. Roast the Pumpkin in the oven, until done.
  2. Mix in the remaining Ingredients and serve.


Black bean Quesadilla


  • 1 can Black beans, rinsed
  • ½ cup Grated Cheese
  • ½ cup prepared fresh salsa
  • 4 8-inch whole-wheat tortillas
  • 2 teaspoons canola oil, divided
  • 1 ripe avocado, diced

  1. Combine beans, cheese and ¼ cup salsa in a medium bowl.
  2. Place tortillas on a work surface. Spread ½ cup filling on half of each tortilla. Fold tortillas in half, pressing gently to flatten.
  3. Heat 1 teaspoon oil in a large nonstick pan over medium heat. Add 2 quesadillas and cook, turning once, until golden on both sides, 2 to 4 minutes total.
  4. Transfer to a cutting board and tent with foil to keep warm.
  5. Repeat with the remaining 1 teaspoon oil and quesadillas.
  6. Serve the quesadillas with avocado and the remaining salsa.
