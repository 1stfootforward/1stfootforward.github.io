Feeling Fuller for longer

When you’re trying to lose weight, cutting back on the amount you eat is a given, but feeling hungry all the time is one of the major reasons why most diets fail within a week. Still, you can silence your grumbling stomach without consuming extra calories. In fact, eating certain foods sends a signal to your brain that you’re done eating and quiets your appetite.

These foods contain a high amount of fiber. Fiber not only aids your digestive track and keeps you regular it also makes you feel fuller for longer.


Vegetable and Bean Chilli

  • 1 tsp Olive oil
  • 1 tsp garlic minced
  • 1 large onion sliced
  • 1 Courgette Sliced
  • 1 Red 1 yellow pepper, deseeded and sliced
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 100g red lentils washed and drained
  • 1 can chopped tomatoes
  • 1 can sweetcorn
  • 1 can Kidney Beans
  • 1 can Butter Beans

  1. In a pan first cook of the onion and the garlic in the olive oil, add in the chili powder, then the lentils, beans and corn coat with the oil, onion, garlic, and spice, cook out for about 3 minutes whilst continuing to stir to avoid it catching burning.
  2. Now add in the tomatoes, add water if the mix is too thick, bring to the boil then simmer for 20 minutes, add in the vegetables and simmer for a further 5 minutes.
  3. This a great dish topped with cheese, sour cream, and Avocado.

Slow cooker veggie lasagne

  • 1 pumpkin, cooked then mashed
  • ¼ tsp ground nutmeg
  • 250 grams ricotta
  • 1 bag baby spinach
  • Salt and Pepper
  • 2 Eggplants finely sliced
  • 2 cup mozzarella, grated
  • 1 cup crushed tomato

  1. In a medium bowl, mix the pumpkin mash and nutmeg. In a second bowl, combine the ricotta, spinach, salt, and pepper.
  2. In the bottom of a slow cooker, spread ½ cup of the pumpkin mixture.
  3. Top with slices of eggplants then the cup of tomato, half the remaining pumpkin mixture, 3 eggplants, and half the ricotta mixture; repeat, ending with the ricotta mixture.
  4. Sprinkle with the mozzarella. Cook on low, covered, until the eggplant is tender, 3 to 4 hours.
  5. Serve with the green salad, if desired.


Chickpea Stuffed Eggplant

  • 2 medium-sized Eggplants. sliced in half lengthwise
  • 1 medium-sized onion, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 cup Chickpea Salad
  • 2 Tbsp. oil
  • 1 small bunch of parsley, minced

  1. Preheat the oven to 180 degrees.
  2. Once the eggplant is sliced in half lengthwise, use a small spoon to scoop out the center. Save the scooped out eggplant and dice it up.
  3. Drizzle about 1 Tbsp of the oil onto the eggplant and place cut side down on a prepared baking sheet.
  4. Cook in the oven for about 15-25 minutes (or until eggplant is evenly cooked and slightly browned on the bottom).
  5. Check often to make sure eggplant doesn't burn. Cook the remaining oil in a large pan.
  6. Add the onion and cook for about 5 minutes.
  7. Add the garlic and cook for about 2 more minutes.
  8. Add the chickpeas, turn heat to low and cook string often (for about 5 more minutes).
  9. Season everything with a little salt and pepper.
  10. Scoop a healthy portion of the chickpea mixture into each eggplant.
  11. Drizzle with a little Greek yogurt and garnish with a few tablespoons of minced parsley.
