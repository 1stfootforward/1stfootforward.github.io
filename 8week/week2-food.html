High Energy Food

I've chosen High Energy food this week to support our keeping active focus. Food is our body’s fuel and without the right fuel, you will find it hard to keep up with your training. Fueling our bodies with the right fuel at the right time of the day will give us the energy we may have never known before.

We more often than not use simple carbohydrates like chocolate bars and soda to give us energy, but however, these will only give you short bursts of energy.

When we fuel our bodies with food our bodies turn the food into blood sugar or glucose. Carbohydrates convert the easiest into this ready to burn fuel, making carbohydrates the macronutrient of choice when eating for energy. However simple carbs like sugar, break down so fast, that after they have provided you with that short boost of energy they just leave your blood sugar low and your energy level inadequate making it hard for your to accomplish your daily activities.

Complex Carbohydrates like grains can replace this spike then dip by giving the body a steady energy supply that keeps you going all day.

Power Protein Salad

Chili Roasted Almonds

  • 1 Cup Sliced Raw Almonds
  • Drizzle Extra Virgin Olive Oil
  • 1/2 Tsp Smoked Paprika
  • 1/2 Tsp Chilli Powder
  • 1/2 Tsp Salt

The Rest of the Salad

  • 1 cup green beans
  • 1 Cup Quinoa cooked
  • 1/2 Cup Black Beans cooked
  • 1/2 Cup Chickpeas cooked
  • 1/4 Red Onion sliced

To Serve

  • Balsamic vinegar & Olive oil
  • Any other nuts and seeds you like

Chili Roasted Almonds

  1. Start by toasting the almonds. While the pan is heating, combine almonds, olive oil, and spices in a bowl. Just use enough olive oil to lightly coat the almonds, not anymore.
  2. Mix well until almonds are evenly coated with the oil and spices. Put in the pan and toast.

The Rest of the Salad

  1. Assemble your salad - toss mixed greens, quinoa, beans, and onion with your favorite salad dressing until evenly combined. This salad is really versatile - feel free to sub quinoa with any other grain.


Sweet Potato Toast

Banana Bread

  • 2 eggs
  • ½ cup coconut oil
  • 1/3 cup maple syrup
  • ¼ cup Greek yoghurt
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 1 cup mashed bananas (about 3)
  • 1 ¼ cup Coconut flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp cinnamon
  • ½ cup Walnuts, chopped

  1. Mix together dry ingredients
  2. In another bowl the wet ingredients add the bananas last then folding in the dry ingredients.
  3. Pour into greased baking dish. Bake for 45 minutes at 180 degrees or until a skewer comes out clean.
  4. Cool for 10 minutes before slicing.


Spiced Barley and Spinach Salad

  • 1 cup Barley
  • 1 tsp each of Paprika, Oregano, Tumeric, Chili powder
  • ¼ cup chopped parsley
  • ¼ cup roasted red peppers
  • 2 Tbsp Slivered Almonds

  1. Cook barley as per packet instructions, once simmering and ½ water is gone add in spices.
  2. When water is all gone and the barley is cooked mix in with the other ingredients.
  3. Serve hot or Cold.


Toasted Muesli

  • 2 cups oats
  • ¼ cup Hazel nuts
  • ¼ cup almonds
  • ¼ cup coconut oil
  • ¼ cup apricots
  • ¼ cup coconut
  • ¼ cup raisins

  1. Heat Oven to 180 degrees, in a baking tray put 2 cups of oats (spread them out to cover bottom)
  2. Brown in the oven for about 5 minutes remove and stir add the nuts and return to the oven for a further 5 minutes repeat the steps with the seeds then add the coconut oil which will be melted and mixed with ½ cup of water. (should be a medium to dry mix) Return to oven for 15 minutes then add fruit and cook for 5 more minutes.
  3. Let cool before storing in an air tight container
