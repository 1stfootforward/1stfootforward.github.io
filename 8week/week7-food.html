Good Fat Recipes

Though it gets a bad rap, fat is an important nutrient that the body needs in order to function. Eating the right amount, and the right form of dietary fat is key to maintaining good health. However, consuming too much fat or too little may cause health problems.

Lemon Herb Salmon Cakes


  • 2 cups sliced peeled parsnips
  • 1 cup diced onion
  • 400g canned salmon
  • 1/4 cup very loosely packed minced parsley
  • 1 Tbsp. fresh minced rosemary
  • 1/2 tsp garlic
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • a few pinches of sea salt
  • 1/4 cup egg whites
  • 1/2 cup whole grain bread crumbs, or ground linseed

  1. Preheat oven to 180 degrees, cook the parsnips until they are fork tender, then mash.
  2. Finely dice onions and add them to the parsnip along with the herbs and salmon.
  3. Season with salt and pepper then add the egg whites and crumbs, roll into balls and cook in the oven for 20 to 25 minutes or cooked all the way through.

NO BAKE MUFFIN BREAKFAST BARS

  • 1 cup dates
  • ¼ cup plus 2 tablespoons almond butter
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1½ tablespoons coconut oil, melted
  • ½ cup oats
  • ½ cup protein powder
  • 1-2 teaspoons water
  • ¼ cup almonds, very roughly chopped
  • ½ cup dark chocolate chips

  1. In a blender, pulse the dates until smooth. Add in the almond butter, maple syrup, and coconut oil. Pulse again until combined.
  2. Transfer to a large mixing bowl. Add in oats, protein powder, and water.
  3. Mix until combined. Fold in almonds and chocolate chips.
  4. Press mixture firmly into a parchment-lined 8"x8" pan. Place in fridge to set for at least 30 minutes.
  5. Once set, remove from fridge and slice into 8 bars. Bars last up to 1 week in the fridge.


Walnut Crusted Salmon

  • 1 Cup Walnuts
  • 2 Tbsp ground linseed
  • 1 Tbsp lemon rind
  • 1 Tbsp olive oil
  • 2 salmon fillets (skin on)
  • 1 tsp dijon mustard
  • 1 Tbsp lemon juice

  1. Place walnuts in food processor; coarsely chop. Add bread crumbs, lemon rind, olive oil and dill; pulse until crumbly. Mixture should stick together. Season; set aside.
  2. Arrange salmon fillets skin side down on parchment paper lined baking sheets. Brush tops with mustard.
  3. Spoon 1/2 of walnut crumb mixture over each fillet, gently press the crumb mixture into the surface of the fish. Cover with plastic wrap; refrigerate for up to 2 hours.
  4. Bake at 180 degrees for 15 to 20 minutes, or until salmon flakes with a fork. Just before serving, sprinkle each with 1 tsp lemon juice.

Avocado Stuffed Boiled Eggs

  • Hard boiled eggs
  • 1 Avocado

  1. In a blender whip up an avocado.
  2. Cut the eggs in halve and remove the yolk.
  3. Scoop the whipped avocado into the egg and serve.
